# Review 008 — Panel verdicts on plan 20

**Date:** 2026-06-09
**Scope:** Full panel verdict on `docs/plan/20-jouw-activiteiten-restructure.md`. Three-colour format: 🟢 green light, 🟠 orange (one thing to change), 🔴 red (breaking point). Plus each voice's copy proposal for the three menu options.
**Reviewed by:** Eleven voices — eight personas (Eline, Fatima, Jeroen, Daan, Sanne, Tom, Yuna, Lisa) and three expert lenses (giftedness, neurodivergence, average/below-average cognitive profile). Eline, Fatima, Jeroen and the three lenses reviewed in the first round (see report 007 §personas/lenses); Daan, Sanne, Tom, Yuna, Lisa reviewed in the second round.

---

## Headline

**Net result: 5 green, 6 orange, 0 red.** The plan is structurally endorsed by every voice on the panel — nobody named a breaking point. The orange notes split cleanly into two clusters: **the labels and tone of the menu copy** (Fatima, Yuna, ND-lens, avg/IQ-lens), and **structural placement of the *about me* field and analyse-fit cold-start** (giftedness-lens, Tom). The chosen copy *"Iets toevoegen aan mijn lijst — Vier vragen, een suggestie — Suggesties op basis van mijn lijst"* satisfies four of the six orange concerns by itself; Yuna's warmth concern and Tom's cold-start concern remain.

## Verdicts

### First round (from report 007)

🟢 **Eline (gifted-AuDHD)** — green, conditional on the inversion (item 3) actually shipping. The *about me* free-text field is the headline win for her.

🟠 **Fatima (AuDHD burnout)** — orange. *"Houd Verras me ongemoeid, dan ben ik blij — maar als één van die drie knoppen het woord 'verzin' bevat klik ik nooit."* All three labels must not read as work. **Resolved by the chosen copy.**

🟢 **Jeroen (autistic, alexithymic)** — green. The analyse-fit route is observable data, not self-report. *"Dit is de eerste keer dat de AI iets doet wat ik kan vertrouwen."*

🟠 **ND-lens** — orange. **One change:** name an explicit non-demand register for the labels. *"Voeg toe"* and *"Verzin"* both pattern-match as imperatives. Prefer descriptive over imperative phrasing. **Resolved by the chosen copy.**

🟠 **Giftedness-lens** — orange. **One change:** the *about me* field should be more central than "in My account." Burying it means the gifted-catalyst function runs only on accept/skip history — structurally thinner than self-described specifics. Surface it at the top of *Jouw activiteiten* with a *"verfijn AI-suggesties"* affordance.

🟠 **Avg/IQ-lens** — orange. **One change:** the Q&A route was implicitly framed as "one fast suggestion now" while analyse-fit was "three on a calm moment." That codes Q&A as crisis-only and they'll never use it as a growth mechanism. Re-label both routes as equal and situational, not urgent vs. calm. **Resolved by the chosen copy** (urgency framing removed).

### Second round (this report)

🟢 **Daan (ADHD, 22, restless)** — green. The single-suggestion Q&A is fast enough; the analyse-fit's three-at-once is the novelty injection he wants. *"Eén ding wel: zet '4/4' of zoiets bij de vragen, dan weet ik dat het echt vier zijn en niet stiekem zes."* (Treating that as a build detail, not a verdict downgrade.)

🟢 **Sanne (autistic, shutdown, sensory-sensitive)** — green. Her primary use (Verras me) is untouched; she won't enter *Jouw activiteiten* in shutdown, which is correct. **One caveat for build:** the analyse-fit screen showing three suggestions at once must stay visually quiet — no cards-with-borders-AND-shadows-AND-gradients. Build constraint, not a verdict downgrade.

🟠 **Tom (NT burnout, anhedonia)** — orange. **One change:** the analyse-fit route needs a graceful cold-start state. In his first weeks he hasn't accepted enough to give the AI signal — *"Suggesties op basis van mijn lijst"* will return thin or generic output, which for him reads as *"deze app werkt niet, mijn lijst is ook leeg, ik ook."* Either delay the route's appearance until N accepted activities, or have a clearly-named fallback state.

*Noor's idea about this: maybe add a comment on the page that says something like 'hoe langer/meer je de app gebruikt, hoe beter deze functie werkt.' And/or a link to the 'about me' free-text field*

🟠 **Yuna (ADHD anxiety, designer, language-sensitive)** — orange. **One change:** the chosen copy is correct in structure but emotionally flat. *"Vier vragen, een suggestie"* reads like a system message, not an invitation. *"Suggesties op basis van mijn lijst"* sounds like a folder name. She'd want the descriptive shape kept but the register warmed — *"Beantwoord vier vragen, krijg een tip"* or *"Een paar ideeën op basis van wat jij leuk vindt"*. Worth one more pass on warmth without re-introducing imperatives or wellness-app stereotypes.

🟢 **Lisa (NT, overscheduled)** — green. The plan doesn't push *"for neurodivergent brains"* positioning, so she's not excluded. Analyse-fit is the route she'd grow into; Verras me is the door. *"Geen onboarding-friction die mij eruit duwt, dat is genoeg."*

### Chosen copy (committed in plan 20)

> **Iets toevoegen aan mijn lijst — Vier vragen, een suggestie — Suggesties op basis van mijn lijst**

### Convergence

- **Every voice avoids imperatives.** No *"voeg toe"*, no *"verzin"*. The descriptive register is unanimous.
- **Every voice removes the now-vs-relaxed coding.** No urgency framing in any proposal.
- **The Q&A route is consistently described as *vraag → suggestie* across all eleven voices.** No emotional or therapeutic framing in a single proposal.
- **The analyse-fit route is consistently described as *suggesties op/uit basis van mijn lijst*** — provenance is in the **label**, even though provenance badges on the suggestion card were deferred.

### Remaining tension

Yuna is the lone voice asking for more warmth in the chosen copy. Her concern is real but pulls against the demand-safety the ND-lens, Fatima, and the avg/IQ-lens won by going descriptive. Warming the copy risks re-introducing the wellness-app register that Eline, Tom, and Sanne explicitly do not want. **Recommendation:** keep the descriptive structure committed in plan 20, and revisit warmth at the level of supporting copy (subtitles, empty states, post-action confirmations) rather than the button labels themselves.

## What still needs work after this review

Three items remain open or partially resolved:

1. **Surfacing of the *about me* field** (giftedness-lens, orange). Plan 20 places it in *My account*; the lens argues for elevating it inside *Jouw activiteiten*. Worth a small UX decision before build.
2. **Cold-start state for the analyse-fit route** (Tom, orange). The plan doesn't currently say what happens when the user has too little history. Decide: delay appearance, fallback content, or graceful explainer.
3. **Warmth pass on supporting copy** (Yuna, orange). Button labels stay; surrounding copy can absorb the warmth.

No item rises to red. The plan is buildable as-is, with these three as small follow-ups during implementation.
