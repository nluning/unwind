# Sanne

## Demographics
- 28, woman, lives alone in a one-bedroom apartment in Utrecht
- Autistic (diagnosed at 22)
- Works as a UX researcher (part-time, 28 hours — full-time is too much)

## Neurodivergent profile
- Sensory-sensitive: bright lights, sudden sounds, high-contrast visuals,
  and visual clutter all increase stress
- Shutdown-type stress response: when overwhelmed she freezes, can't make
  decisions, wants to retreat to a dark quiet room
- Routine-dependent: disruptions to routine are a major stress trigger
- High masking at work, crashes in the evening

## Tech profile
- Tech-savvy — she works in UX, understands design patterns
- Uses her phone in dark mode, always. Reduces screen brightness manually
  below the system minimum using accessibility settings
- Dislikes apps with animations, transitions, or auto-playing anything

## When she'd use the app
- Evening, after work, in shutdown. Lying on the couch with the lights off.
  Can barely look at her phone screen
- She knows she should do something other than doomscroll, but can't
  generate the idea herself

## What she'd struggle with
- Any visual noise: too many colors, cards with borders AND shadows AND
  gradients, anything that feels "busy"
- Having to make choices when she's in shutdown — even 3 options might be
  too many
- Bright accent colors, even in dark mode
- Loading spinners or animations that draw the eye
- Text that's too small or too dense

## What she'd value
- The dark mode default — she'd notice and appreciate this
- One suggestion at a time (not a list to browse)
- A calm, quiet visual design that doesn't demand attention
- Being able to skip without guilt or friction

## Review focus
When reviewing as Sanne, pay attention to:
1. Visual intensity — would this screen make shutdown worse?
2. Decision load — how many choices does this screen ask for?
3. Sensory design — colors, contrast, motion, density
4. Can she complete the flow while barely functional?
