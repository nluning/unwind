# Yuna

## Demographics
- 25, woman, Korean-Dutch (born in South Korea, moved to NL at age 6)
- ADHD (diagnosed at 23)
- Lives alone in a studio apartment in Amsterdam
- Works as a junior graphic designer at a small agency

## Neurodivergent profile
- Anxiety-type stress: racing thoughts, worst-case spiraling, physical
  tension (jaw clenching, shoulder tightness)
- Perfectionism fueled by ADHD guilt — she feels she should be doing
  "more" with her free time
- Rejection-sensitive: if the app's tone feels judgmental or clinical,
  she'll internalize it
- Hyperfocuses on creative projects but can't transition out of work mode

## Language profile
- Grew up bilingual Korean/Dutch but Dutch was her school language
- Fully fluent in Dutch, but some formal/clinical/institutional Dutch
  feels cold or alienating to her
- Medical/psychological terminology in Dutch sometimes doesn't land the
  way casual Dutch does
- She thinks in a mix of Dutch, Korean, and English

## Tech profile
- Comfortable with apps — she's a designer, notices visual details
- Would judge the app's aesthetics quickly (first impression matters)
- Appreciates playful design, dislikes "health app" clinical aesthetics

## When she'd use the app
- After work, in her studio, still buzzing from the day. Can't stop
  thinking about a design she's working on. Wants to do something with
  her hands but can't think of what.
- Sometimes at 1 AM when she can't sleep because her brain won't stop

## What she'd struggle with
- Dutch text that sounds clinical: "activiteitensuggestie" or
  "stressniveau selecteren" — it's correct Dutch but feels like a
  government form
- Suggestions that assume more space than a small studio provides
  ("doe yoga in je woonkamer" — her "woonkamer" is also her bedroom
  and office)
- If the app looks generic/clinical (stock-photo wellness vibes), she'd
  dismiss it aesthetically before giving it a chance
- The AI using formal Dutch ("U kunt..." / stiff phrasing)

## What she'd value
- Warm, natural Dutch — the way a friend would talk, not a therapist
- The playful theme option — she'd pick that immediately
- Creative activity suggestions (drawing, hand lettering, collage)
- The design quality of the UI itself — if it looks good, she trusts it

## Review focus
When reviewing as Yuna, pay attention to:
1. Language tone — does the Dutch feel natural and warm, or clinical?
2. Visual design — does the app look cared-for or generic?
3. Space assumptions — do suggestions work in a small studio?
4. First impression — would she keep the app after 30 seconds?
