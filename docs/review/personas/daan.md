# Daan

## Demographics
- 22, man, university student (computer science, 2nd year)
- ADHD (diagnosed at 16, medicated with methylphenidate but it wears off
  by evening)
- Lives in a shared student house with 4 housemates

## Neurodivergent profile
- Restless/wired stress response: when stressed he can't sit still, opens
  and closes apps, starts things and abandons them within minutes
- Impulsive: taps fast, doesn't read instructions, skips onboarding flows
- Novelty-seeking: gets bored of the same suggestions quickly
- Executive function drops sharply in the evening when meds wear off
- Hyperfocuses on games/coding but can't start "relaxation" activities

## Tech profile
- Very comfortable with tech — CS student, builds side projects
- Impatient with slow or clunky apps. If something takes more than 2 taps
  to do, he'll close the app
- Doesn't read help text, tooltips, or onboarding screens — taps through
  them to get to the actual app

## When he'd use the app
- Late evening, meds worn off, wired but exhausted. He knows he should
  sleep but his brain won't stop. Scrolling between apps restlessly.
- Sometimes mid-afternoon during a procrastination spiral

## What he'd struggle with
- Slow flows — if the app takes more than a few seconds to suggest
  something, he's gone
- Repetitive suggestions — if he sees "ga wandelen" three times in a week,
  the app feels useless
- Too much text in the chat interface — he won't read long AI messages
- Onboarding that takes more than 60 seconds
- Having to self-assess stress level precisely (he'd just pick a random
  number to get through it)

## What he'd value
- Speed — one tap, one suggestion, done
- Variety in suggestions
- The counterbalance mode might actually work for him (he does too much
  "Head" stuff and needs "Hands" or "Heart")
- Short, punchy AI responses in Mode 4

## Review focus
When reviewing as Daan, pay attention to:
1. Speed — how many taps/seconds to get a suggestion?
2. Friction — anything that would make him close the app?
3. Repetition — would suggestions feel stale after a week?
4. Text density — would he actually read what's on screen?
