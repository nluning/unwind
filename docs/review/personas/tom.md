# Tom

## Demographics
- 38, man, lives with his partner and 8-year-old daughter in Amersfoort
- Neurotypical, but recovering from burnout (6 months off work, now
  slowly reintegrating at 16 hours/week)
- Was a team lead at a logistics company. Currently seeing a psychologist
  biweekly.

## Profile
- His burnout manifests as: flat affect, low motivation, difficulty
  experiencing pleasure (anhedonia), brain fog, fatigue
- Not depressed in the clinical sense, but many symptoms overlap
- His psychologist suggested finding small activities that bring micro-
  moments of enjoyment — but he can't think of any, and the things he
  used to enjoy (cycling, cooking, reading) feel like too much effort
- He's skeptical of apps but willing to try because his usual strategies
  aren't working

## Tech profile
- Average tech skills — uses standard apps, nothing special
- Low patience for setup or complexity right now (brain fog)
- Won't explore features on his own — he'll use whatever the app shows
  him first
- Might not discover Mode 3 or Mode 4 unless the navigation is obvious

## When he'd use the app
- Afternoon, home alone while his partner is at work and daughter is at
  school. He's supposed to "rest" but sitting on the couch feels awful.
  He wants to do *something* but has no idea what and no energy to decide.
- His psychologist might literally say "try that app we talked about"

## What he'd struggle with
- The stress level selector: his stress isn't acute like the ND personas
  — it's a flat, chronic low-grade exhaustion. Is that a 2? A 4? The
  scale doesn't map to his experience.
- Suggestions that require motivation or energy he doesn't have:
  "start een creatief project" feels insurmountable
- If the AI is too cheerful or encouraging ("Wat leuk dat je iets wilt
  doen!") — it would feel patronizing when he's barely functional
- The word "ontspannen" — he's not tense, he's empty. Relaxation isn't
  the issue, activation is.

## What he'd value
- Very low-effort suggestions with tiny scope ("luister 10 minuten naar
  muziek", "kijk uit het raam en tel de vogels die je ziet")
- Mode 1 (just give me something) — he can't evaluate options right now
- If the app doesn't assume he's stressed/overstimulated. His problem
  is the opposite: understimulated, flat, can't engage
- Neutral, non-therapeutic tone — he gets enough therapy talk from his
  psychologist

## Review focus
When reviewing as Tom, pay attention to:
1. Energy assumptions — does the app assume "stressed" means
   "overstimulated"? Tom is the opposite.
2. Tone — is it warm without being therapeutic or patronizing?
3. Activation vs. relaxation — are there suggestions for someone who
   needs to *start* doing something, not *stop*?
4. Discoverability — would he find the useful features without exploring?
