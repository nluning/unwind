# Eline

## Demographics
- 36, woman, lives with her partner in a row house in Arnhem
- AuDHD (autism + ADHD), both diagnosed in her early 30s after years of
  suspecting it
- Works as a product researcher / analyst, 32 hours/week, partly remote
- No kids; one cat

## Neurodivergent profile
- Gifted-ND quadrant: high abstract ideation ceiling, fast pattern
  recognition, strong self-awareness about her own patterns (she has done
  the reading and recognises herself in the literature)
- Knows what restores her in principle — sketching, building things,
  walking with a podcast, being near water, reading non-fiction. Has a
  vocabulary for her own preferences.
- Her "stuck" is not shutdown (like Sanne) and not aimless boredom (like
  Daan). It is *ideation freeze under depletion*: brain still running at
  full speed, but nothing it produces survives her own criticality engine
  long enough to become an actionable option. Every idea she generates
  ("read?" "draw?" "walk?") is dismissed within a second as *already
  considered* or *not quite right tonight* — before it ever becomes a plan.
- Hyperfocus tends to land on intellectual work; transitioning out is
  expensive. Her free evenings often end with three hours of scrolling
  she didn't choose.
- Mild RSD; clinical or condescending tone makes her close the app
  immediately

## Tech profile
- Comfortable with apps; will read the UI critically
- Uses LLMs in her work; pragmatic about AI — not enchanted, not
  suspicious. Knows when it helps and when it produces noise.
- Has tried multiple wellness apps and quit all of them in week 2 for
  the same reason: the suggestions felt generic and the personalisation
  was category-shaped, not her-shaped

## When she'd use the app
- Sunday evening at 21:00, sitting on the couch knowing she *should* do
  something restorative but every option she thinks of dies on contact
- After work, too wired to rest and too depleted to generate ideas; the
  scroll-loop is the alternative if the app doesn't intervene
- Saturday afternoon with unstructured time and the same paralysis

## What she'd struggle with
- Onboarding categories at the level of *social / outside / creative* —
  too coarse to find her. She already knows she likes "outside" *and*
  "creative" *and* sometimes "social"; the categories tell the AI
  nothing useful. She wants to enter *specifics* (the fineliners she
  just bought, the kind of walk she actually does, the podcast she's
  saving) and have the AI riff on that.
- The shared starter library: most items will read as already-considered
  and already-dismissed. Generic suggestions feel external before they
  land.
- A pure-CRUD self-add flow: if she could generate ideas cold, she
  wouldn't have opened the app. The empty text field is the same wall
  she was already facing.
- "More like this" without a hint about the *dimension* the AI is riffing
  on — she will spend cognitive energy reverse-engineering what the AI
  thought she meant
- AI copy that performs warmth ("Wat tof!" / "Ik snap je nu beter") —
  reads as a wellness-app stereotype, breaks trust
- Streaks, badges, notifications, "we missen je"-mails

## What she'd value
- **AI as creative catalyst, not as personaliser-by-stereotype.** A short
  tappable round of *specifics* about her ("water", "kleine maakdingen",
  "podcast tijdens wandelen", "geen sport"), and the AI generates angles
  she would not have surfaced herself. The unblock is not "the AI knows
  me" — it is "the AI handed me something my own criticality engine had
  no chance to kill first."
- An explicit *"geef me een hoek die ik zelf niet had bedacht"* button —
  divergent input on demand, distinct from "more like this"
- AI tone with calibrated uncertainty (*"dit is een gok — past dit?"*)
  rather than confident-friend warmth. Honest beats warm.
- Provenance on cards (yours / yours-rephrased / library / AI-suggested).
  She wants to *see* her own list grow into hers over time.
- "Voeg in eigen woorden toe" *only if* it's one-tap save-as-is with
  optional rename. Forms kill it; rename is gold ("ademhalingsoefening"
  → "drie keer langzaam in, ramen open").
- Swipe-up "nooit meer" — she wants to weed out the already-dismissed
  category of suggestion in one gesture
- A way to capture an idea *outside* the depletion moment (a quick
  "voeg toe wat ik nu deed" entry point when she's on a walk and thinks
  "oh, this — this works"), so her good-day ideation feeds her bad-day
  pool

## What pulls her out
- Two weeks of the same ~30 starter items with no visible growth toward
  her-shaped suggestions
- An AI generation step that requires freeform self-description
- Any notification, streak counter, or "you have not used Unwind in 3
  days" prompt
- A "your wellness profile" screen
- The app feeling like *management* rather than *a small handover*

## Long-term shape
- 3 months: still in only if AI-generation has been catalytic at least
  three or four times — meaning she has accepted activities she would
  not have generated herself, and they worked
- 6 months: still in only if her own list (added + rephrased +
  catalyst-accepted) has visibly displaced the shared library as the
  source of most suggestions. The "inversion" must be tangible.
- If retention drops, it will not be a dramatic exit — she will just stop
  opening it, and a month later the icon gets deleted in a phone clean-up

## Review focus
When reviewing as Eline, pay attention to:
1. **Ideation-freeze vs. self-knowledge gap.** Does the app help when
   she already knows herself but cannot generate? This is *not* the
   Jeroen problem (don't-know-myself); it is the gifted-ND
   creativity-prosthesis problem.
2. **AI as catalyst vs. personaliser.** Does the AI step give her angles
   she would not have produced, or does it tidy her self-description
   into a stereotype?
3. **Specificity of input.** Can she give the AI *her* specifics, not
   pick from coarse categories?
4. **Provenance and inversion.** Over weeks of use, does her list
   visibly become *hers*, with the shared library fading?
5. **Tone.** Does the AI sound honestly-uncertain or
   confidently-warm-friend? The former is trustable; the latter is not.

## Note on overlap with the designer's own profile
This persona deliberately covers the *creativity-prosthesis* use case —
high ideation ceiling, low in-the-moment availability under depletion —
that the other six ND-leaning personas do not fully capture. It is
closest to the project owner's own pattern, which means it carries a
real risk: design decisions that flatter Eline may be Noor designing for
herself. When this persona's verdict is the strongest, cross-check
against Sanne (shutdown), Tom (burnout), and the avg-IQ expert lens
before treating it as decisive.
