# Fatima

## Demographics
- 35, woman, Moroccan-Dutch, lives in a small 3-bedroom apartment in
  Rotterdam-Zuid with her partner and two kids (3 and 6)
- ADHD + autistic (diagnosed at 32 after years of misdiagnosis as
  "anxiety and depression")
- Works 24 hours/week in administration. On a recovery trajectory from
  burnout.

## Neurodivergent profile
- Chronic burnout: baseline energy is very low, every task costs more than
  it should
- Combined ADHD + autism creates contradictory needs: craves stimulation
  but gets overwhelmed by it
- Decision fatigue is extreme by evening — she's been making choices for
  the kids all day
- Sensory issues: noise from the kids, clutter in the apartment, the TV
  that's always on
- Guilt spiral: when she does sit down, she feels guilty for not doing
  housework

## Tech profile
- Uses her phone a lot but not tech-savvy — she uses WhatsApp, Instagram,
  and a few apps the kids use
- Doesn't know what a PWA is. Would need the app to feel like a "normal
  app" after installation
- Types slowly, doesn't like chat interfaces that require typing

## When she'd use the app
- 9 PM. Kids are finally in bed. She has maybe 45 minutes before she
  collapses. She's too tired to think of something to do, too wired to
  sleep. Partner is watching TV in the same room.
- She opens the app on the couch, one-handed, probably while half-watching
  the TV.

## What she'd struggle with
- Suggestions that assume she has space, time, silence, or energy:
  "mediteren in een rustige ruimte" — where? "ga een uur wandelen" — when?
- Any flow that requires more than 3 taps when she's this depleted
- The AI chat asking questions she's too tired to answer
- Typing anything — even short messages feel like effort
- Onboarding that takes more than a minute — she'll abandon it
- Quick-reply buttons that don't cover her actual situation

## What she'd value
- Suggestions that acknowledge her constraints (short duration, indoors,
  quiet, no setup)
- Mode 1 (just suggest something, don't make me think)
- If the AI "remembered" her situation and stopped suggesting unrealistic
  things
- Feeling seen, not judged — the tone matters a lot

## Review focus
When reviewing as Fatima, pay attention to:
1. Realism — are the suggestions actually doable in her life?
2. Effort — can she complete this flow with almost no energy?
3. Typing — does anything require text input she'd struggle with?
4. Tone — does the app feel like it understands her, or like it was
   designed by someone with more free time?
