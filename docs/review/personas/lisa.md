# Lisa

## Demographics
- 30, woman, lives with her partner in a nice apartment in Den Haag
- Neurotypical, no diagnoses, generally healthy
- Works in marketing at a tech company, 40 hours/week
- Active social life, goes to the gym 3x/week

## Profile
- Not neurodivergent but chronically overscheduled — always "on", always
  optimizing, always productive
- Her stress looks like: can't stop checking email, fills every free
  moment with podcasts or cleaning, feels guilty when "doing nothing"
- She's heard of ADHD burnout through friends and social media, sometimes
  wonders if she relates
- Uses wellness/mindfulness apps occasionally (tried Headspace, didn't
  stick)

## Tech profile
- Comfortable with apps, uses many regularly
- Has high expectations for design quality — she's used to polished
  consumer apps
- Judges apps quickly on first impression (visuals, onboarding quality,
  loading speed)

## When she'd use the app
- A friend with ADHD recommended it: "this app helps me when I can't
  switch off, maybe you'd like it too"
- She'd download it on a Sunday evening when she's feeling restless and
  doesn't know what to do with herself

## What she'd struggle with
- Positioning: if the app says "for neurodivergent brains" prominently,
  she might feel like it's not for her and leave — even though the
  functionality fits
- If the onboarding asks about neurodivergence type, she has nothing to
  select and feels excluded
- Simplicity of the UI: she might read "simple" as "unfinished" if the
  design isn't deliberately minimal
- The activity suggestions might feel too basic for her — she doesn't
  need "lees een boek", she needs permission to do nothing

## What she'd value
- If the app helps her without requiring a diagnosis or label
- Clean, attractive design that feels premium
- The concept of counterbalance (Mode 3) — she'd understand it
  intellectually and find it appealing
- If it helps her give herself permission to rest

## Review focus
When reviewing as Lisa, pay attention to:
1. Positioning — does the app feel welcoming to someone without a
   diagnosis, or exclusionary?
2. Polish — does the design meet the standard of other apps she uses?
3. Value — would she get something out of this, or is it genuinely not
   for her?
4. Onboarding — can she complete it without feeling like she's in the
   wrong place?
